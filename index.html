Feature overview - what is Semasia about?

Even if you are not an expert in Machine Learning (ML) or Artificial Intelligence (AI), you are probably aware that recent advances in Natural Language Processing (NLP) are making it easier for computers to “read” and process text data. There are so many different text analytics tools and services out there that even for the technology savvy, it can be difficult to quickly tell them apart from an accuracy and speed standpoint...

Semasia is different because of its exclusive focus on a particularly difficult NLP task: reliable Entity Linking at scale. It does not replace other NLP tools, but complements them by filling this important gap. Semasia is not going to help you with basic NLP tasks or with sentiment analysis...
If you are after state-of-the-art Entity Linking, you are definitely in the right place!

Without
Semasia

At the risk of oversimplifying: computers usually convert text into mathematical vectors in order to represent language. The size and shape of these vectors are specific to every NLP model - and since people don't speak "vector", a final conversion from vectors back to language is often necessary.

A computer can easily detect that "cat" and "dog" have similar vector representations. Yet, it cannot infer from the vectors alone that while they are both mammals, one belongs to the Felidae family and the other to the Canis familiy - or that a dog's life expectancy ranges from 10 to 13 years.

More often than not, significant custom development work is required to integrate text analytics tools into new or existing workflows. As a result, text data is often stored in a raw format and is not immediately ready for consumption - calling for subsequent retrieval and processing instead.

With
Semasia

Semasia acts as a bridge between how computers see text (mathematical vectors) and how people process information (semantic concepts). It's flexible and generic enough to adapt to any vector model and it can instantly provide a normalized view between different models.

Semasia organizes and connects very large amounts of semantic concepts and data points, from generic real-world knowledge to information that is specific and exclusive to your business. Multiple hierarchies and relationships between concepts can co-exist at the same time.

Unlike many NLP tools that target developers, Semasia is designed with people in mind who manage and use data. Semasia integrates seamlessly with your existing data management processes and flows using connectors rather than custom code. Availability: Spring 2020.

Without
Semasia

In most cases, text data is stored in a raw format or is sometimes indexed to be searchable. However, there are major limitations to such an approach: it's challenging to achieve hierarchy and grouping consistency between structured and unstructured data, and there is little to no flexibility to re-organize unstructured data.

Data preparation and labeling, and the subsequent feature engineering, are the most critical and time-consuming tasks of any data science project... especially so for unstructured data. When data is not pre-encoded, as is often the case, all these tasks happen on a project by project basis.

With
Semasia

Semasia can bring structure to text data using customizable organization principles that mirror the configuration of the data catalog. It can generate a structured data version of a text document that can be more productively ingested. Perhaps more critically, it unlocks the usefulness and value of a large data trove that was largely left untouched.

By integrating Semasia in your main unstructured data pipeline, highly detailed and reconfigurable labels are immediately available to data scientists. They can immediately explore potential features and avoid wasting precious time and costly resources processing information that will not be relevant to the final result.

How Semasia came about


From survey verbatims to call center transcripts and social media conversations, our team has been at the forefront of text analytics for almost two decades.

Over the last few years, we focused our efforts on the puzzling problem of word sense ambiguity and entity linking - which in turn, led to the development of technology that can process and organize large volume of unstructured text data such as Wikipedia.

It eventually became apparent that these were in fact the two sides of a same coin... Semasia (the Greek word for "meaning") is a unique technological combination: on one hand, text analytics to bring out accurate, detailed meaning and on the other hand, a relationship graph that links and organizes this meaning.

Semasia is headquartered in New York City, with team members in two slightly more exotic locations.
